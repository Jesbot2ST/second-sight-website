The execution gap is real — and validated.

"Australian AI ambitions are strong, but it is time to turn intent into action." AI investment averages $28M annually per enterprise, yet 72% report failing to achieve measurable ROI — and the shortfall in mid-market firms is 71%.

ADAPT Research — State of Data & AI in Australia, 2025

Deloitte's 2026 data provides the starkest Australia-versus-global comparison: just 12% of Australian leaders report that generative AI is already transforming their business, compared to 25% globally — running at half the global transformation rate. Only 24% possess AI-ready data architectures, and only 6% mandate enterprise-wide AI training.

The Cisco AI Readiness Index 2024 confirms only 4% of Australian businesses are fully prepared to deploy AI — down from 5% the prior year despite 92% acknowledging heightened urgency. 64% of organisations have provided zero AI training and 93% cannot effectively measure AI ROI. The cost of inaction is estimated at A$142 billion in economic opportunity at risk.

Purchase Triggers

  • Failed internal AI pilot
  • Competitor visibly gaining AI advantage
  • Board mandate with a specific deadline
  • Shadow AI security incident
  • New CTO joining the organisation
  • Strategic planning cycle / post-merger integration
  • Departure of the sole internal "AI person"

What Buyers Need to Believe

  • The consultant understands their specific context (not generic AI hype)
  • Tangible, measurable outcomes will result
  • The investment will demonstrably pay for itself
  • The workshop won't disrupt operations
  • The consultant has done this with companies like theirs (AU case studies essential)
  • Their team will be upskilled, not made dependent on external help

Typical Decision-Making Unit (50–500 employees)

CTO/CPO — champion and initiator  |  CEO/MD — final decision-maker  |  CFO — budget gatekeeper  |  Head of Engineering/Product — technical validator

Below $10K: CTO may have discretionary authority. At $10K–$25K: one additional approval (CEO or CFO). Above $25K: formal CEO + CFO sign-off almost certain.

Top 5 Objections to Anticipate

  • "We have no allocated budget" — 15% of SMBs create budgets at point of decision; AI often has no dedicated line item
  • "We can figure this out ourselves" — especially from technically proud CTOs
  • "Let's wait until next quarter" — timing deflection when there is no acute trigger
  • "We've had bad experiences with consultants before" — fear of generic advice and shelf-ware deliverables
  • "That's more than we expected to spend" — sticker shock at $25K+ end without pre-anchoring on ROI

Sales cycle estimate: 4–10 weeks from first meaningful conversation to signed engagement. The shorter end (4–6 weeks) applies with a strong referral, acute pain point, and single decision-maker with budget authority. The longer end (8–12+ weeks) applies when multiple stakeholders are involved or CFO approval is required. Note: sales cycles have lengthened 22% since 2022 (Optifai) due to increased budget scrutiny.

⚠ Assumptions Not Fully Supported by Evidence

  • The 50–500 employee segment is assumed to behave distinctly from smaller SMBs and larger enterprises, but most Australian AI adoption data covers either end. The mid-market sits in a measurement gap.
  • The assumption that these businesses "already have an AI strategy on paper" is partially challenged — fewer than two-thirds of the target market have a clear AI plan (Cisco), and 23% of SMEs remain unaware of AI's potential applications (NAIC).
  • The assumption that a 1–2 day workshop can meaningfully close the execution gap is untested in Australia — the UK's most successful model (Twist Consultancy) runs 12 weeks, and global evidence suggests pilot-to-production transitions require sustained support.

Prioritised Assessment

Top 5 Risks to the Offer Hypothesis

1

The Workshop-to-Implementation Gap

The strongest risk is that a 1–2 day workshop delivers a roadmap but does not ensure execution — replicating the very problem Second Sight claims to solve. With 72% of Australian enterprises failing to achieve AI ROI (ADAPT), buyers are primed to be sceptical of yet another deliverable that sits on a shelf. The Twist Consultancy 12-week model and AI Accelerator UK's ecosystem suggest the market may demand ongoing engagement, not a standalone event.

Build 30/60/90-day implementation support into the product from day one
2

Thin Mid-Market Evidence Base

The 50–500 employee mid-market is underserved by Australian research. Second Sight's assumptions are reasonable extrapolations but not directly validated. The segment may be internally heterogeneous — a 60-person tech startup and a 400-person manufacturing firm have very different AI needs, buying processes, and budget authorities.

Conduct primary research with 15–20 target buyers before finalising the offer
3

Competitive Convergence

While the mid-market gap currently exists, it is visible to every competitor. Mantel Group, Thoughtworks, AirStack AI, AI Surge, and the Big 4 are all investing in AI services. The barrier to entry for "a 1–2 day AI workshop" is low — any established consultancy could launch a comparable product within weeks. The ex-Accenture credential differentiates initially but is not defensible long-term.

Move fast; build proprietary frameworks and IP that create switching costs
4

Price Sensitivity & Sales Cycle Friction

At $25,000–$50,000, the engagement requires CEO/CFO approval and competes with hiring a contractor for 2–3 months, purchasing AI SaaS tools, or running internal experiments with free tools. Sales cycles of 8–12 weeks at this price point strain a boutique firm's cash flow and pipeline predictability. 15% of SMBs create budgets at time of purchase decision — AI consulting often lacks a pre-existing budget line.

Anchor at $15–20K; design a $5–8K diagnostic entry point; develop compelling ROI framing
5

Some Target Buyers May Need Strategy, Not Activation

23% of Australian SMEs remain unaware of AI's potential applications (NAIC), and 42% have no plans to implement. Cisco found fewer than two-thirds have a clear AI plan. This means a meaningful portion of the addressable market is not yet at the "we have a strategy but can't execute" stage — they may need foundational education or strategy development first. Positioning exclusively around "activation" could narrow the addressable market more than intended.

Design to accommodate multiple maturity stages; consider a lighter AI Foundations diagnostic
